<jats:title>Abstract</jats:title>
               <jats:p>Results of photometric and polarimetric observations of OJ 287 are presented. It is confirmed that colors remained constant throughout the recent declining phase, although short-term variations of colors were found as in the active phase. The strength of polarization at the position angle 90° increased with time as Kinman et al. (1974) suggested. The mean polarized flux is almost the same in both active and basic phases, and it is suggested that an ordered magnetic field is constantly present in the source. The relationship of the polarimetric properties of OJ 287 with those of other BL Lac-type objects, especially with those of BL Lac itself, is also discussed.</jats:p>
